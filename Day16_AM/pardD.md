Prompt

Explain Type I and Type II errors in the context of a fraud detection system. 
What are the real-world consequences of each, and how do you balance them?

---

AI Explanation (summary)

Type I error occurs when a legitimate transaction is flagged as fraud. 
This creates customer friction and may lead to lost trust.

Type II error occurs when fraudulent activity is not detected. 
This leads to financial losses and potential legal issues.

Fraud systems must balance these errors. 
If the threshold is too strict, many legitimate transactions are blocked. 
If it is too lenient, fraud passes through.

---

Evaluation

The explanation correctly connects Type I errors to false positives 
and Type II errors to false negatives.

It also implicitly relates to the precision–recall tradeoff: 
reducing false positives increases precision, while detecting more fraud increases recall.
