A violin plot is used when we want to see the full distribution shape in addition to summary statistics.

A box plot only shows the median, quartiles, and outliers. A violin plot also shows the kernel density, which reveals multimodal distributions or skewness.


Q3

a) A 3D pie chart with 12 segments is difficult to read because angles are hard to compare and the 3D effect distorts proportions.

Better alternative: a horizontal bar chart sorted by market share.


b) A line chart for unordered categories is misleading because lines imply a continuous order or progression.

Better alternative: a bar chart.


c) A scatter plot with 500k points causes overplotting and hides patterns.

Better alternative: use hexbin plots, transparency (alpha), or density plots.
